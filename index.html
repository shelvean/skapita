Research interests and background

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Mathematics and Computational Sciences at the University of Zimbabwe. I received my PhD in Applied Mathematics in 2016 from the Department of Mathematical Sciences at The University of Delaware.

I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at the IMA , University of Minnesota from August 2016 to August 2017. I was also a Limited Term Assistant Professor at the University of Georgia from 2017 to June 2020

My research focuses on numerical analysis and scientific computation. I am particularly interested in the design and application of numerical methods to partial differential equations that arise in science and engineering applications. Recently I have been thinking about Bernstein-Bezier and multivariate spline methods for solving variational inequalities, particularly the elliptic obstacle problem. Special interests include numerical PDE constrained optimization, numerical methods for wave propagation, artificial boundary conditions for unbounded frequency domain problems, and discontinuous Galerkin methods.
My papers and preprints are on my research page.
